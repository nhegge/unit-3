Cost of Living Across the Midwest & Great Lakes Region (2024)

An interactive application to explore and compare income, rent, unemployment, and resident demographics across 16 states

As inflation only continues to rise, cost of living has become a major concern across the United States. This visualization explores major factors (attributes) influencing the cost of living in the Midwest and Great Lakes region of the United States in 2024. The attributes depicted in this visualization are: median household income, average rent for a 1 bedroom apartment, unemployment rate, population density per square mile, and average age. You can use the dropdown menu below to select which attribute you would like to visualize on the choropleth map on the left and the bar chart on the right. Hover over any state on the map or bar in the graph to see more detailed information about that State. The bar graph plots the selected attribute on the y-axis and the States (in ascending order based on the selected attribute) from left to right.

Data Sources:

Living expense data: U.S. Census Bureau, American Community Survey (ACS) 5-Year Estimates. | Rent data: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) Fair Market Rents. | Unemployment data: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS). | Population density calculated from: U.S. Census Bureau, State Area Measurements and Internal Point Coordinates (2010), census.gov/geographies/reference-files/2010/geo/state-area.html. | State boundary data: Natural Earth, 1:110m Cultural Vectors, Admin 1 States and Provinces.
